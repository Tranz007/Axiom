Edition strategy

One core. Different trust postures.

Axiom should share one language foundation across a planned open-source release, business governance, and government environments. The repo is private while the local core is hardened.

Axiom OS

Planned open-source release

The local CLI, language spec, examples, policy simulation, generated contracts, and runtime guards. Its job is to prove the model before public release.

Axiom Business

Enterprise governance

Organization policy profiles, SSO, approval workflow integrations, audit dashboards, CI checks, evidence reports, and enterprise connectors.

Axiom Government

High-trust environments

GovCloud and restricted-environment deployment, default-deny profiles, signed bundles, review packs, attestations, and stricter audit defaults.

Why keep the repo private now?

First impressions matter. The public site can explain the direction while the implementation is still being hardened. Opening the repo should happen when the local CLI, examples, packaging, and docs feel like a usable tool instead of a rough research checkout.
